I am a PhD student at the Computer Science Department, University of Maryland, College Park, where I'm advised by Prof. Tom Goldstein. I obtained my Bachelor from Zhejiang University, supervised by Prof. Wei Chen. You can reach me through my-last-name@cs.umd.edu.

At Google I've worked on Portrait Light, Lens Blur, HDR+, Jump, Portrait Mode, Glass, and NeRF. I did my PhD at UC Berkeley, where I was advised by Jitendra Malik and funded by the NSF GRFP. I've received the C.V. Ramamoorthy Distinguished Research Award and the PAMI Young Researcher Award.

Research

I do research in Machine Learning. My recent works focus on Graph Learning, especially to universally enhance the performance and scalability of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). I have also worked in Adversarial Attacks/Defenses, Semi-supervised Learning, Meta Learning, and Medical Imaging. Representative papers are highlighted.
